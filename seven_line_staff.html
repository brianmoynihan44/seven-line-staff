What is an Interval?

An interval is the distance between two notes, measured in semitones (half-steps). In this system, intervals are plain numbers: 0 = the same note, 7 = a perfect fifth higher, 12 = one octave higher.

The Just Intonation ratio expresses the exact frequency relationship as a simple fraction. A perfect fifth (7) is 3/2 — the upper note vibrates 1.5× faster. Simpler ratios sound more consonant. Click any card to see and hear the interval on the staff.
